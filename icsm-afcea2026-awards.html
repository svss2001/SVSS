Recognitions

Awards & Recognitions

To recognize and celebrate excellence in research, innovation, presentation, and academic contribution, the International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications” will confer a set of prestigious awards during the Valedictory Session of the conference. These awards are intended to encourage researchers at different career stages and to promote high-quality, original, and impactful scholarly work aligned with the themes of the conference, particularly in mathematics, fractional calculus, computational methods, nonlinear systems, and engineering applications.

Unless otherwise specified, awards may be considered under broad participant categories such as:

  • Undergraduate / Postgraduate Student
  • Research Scholar / Faculty / Early-Career Researcher

The final number of awards and categories may be determined by the Organizing Committee based on the quality and number of eligible nominations/submissions.

Young Researcher Award

The Young Researcher Award is intended to honor an outstanding early-career researcher who has demonstrated exceptional promise through innovative research contributions in areas related to mathematics, fractional calculus, nonlinear analysis, computational science, mathematical modeling, and engineering applications.

This award is designed to encourage young scholars whose work reflects originality, strong analytical depth, interdisciplinary relevance, and the potential for long-term research impact.

Eligibility

  • Typically open to early-career researchers / young faculty / postdoctoral researchers / research scholars, generally below a specified age limit if prescribed by the conference.
  • The applicant should be an author/co-author of a paper submitted to the conference and should present the work during the conference.
  • The work should align with the themes of the conference.

How to Apply

Authors wishing to be considered for this award should indicate “Paper for Young Researcher Award” at the time of abstract / paper submission, if such an option is provided in the submission process.

Evaluation Criteria

  • originality and novelty of the research;
  • technical quality, scientific rigor, and mathematical depth;
  • clarity of presentation and articulation of results;
  • relevance to the conference themes and potential impact of the work.

Award Benefits

  • Young Researcher Award Certificate
  • Memento / Trophy / Medal
  • Special recognition during the Valedictory Ceremony
  • Cash prize / financial award, if approved by the Organizing Committee
  • Recognition on the conference website / official communication channels
  • Consideration for publication-related recognition or future invited academic engagement, subject to conference policy

Best Paper Award

The Best Paper Award will be presented to the author(s) of the paper judged to be the most original, impactful, technically sound, and well-presented among the accepted and presented papers at the conference.

This award recognizes outstanding scholarly contribution and aims to encourage high-quality research in mathematics, fractional calculus, and engineering applications. In many cases, papers recommended by technical session chairs or review panels may be shortlisted for final evaluation.

Evaluation Criteria

  • originality and significance of the research problem;
  • methodological soundness and technical quality;
  • theoretical depth and/or practical relevance;
  • clarity of writing, organization, and presentation;
  • contribution to the advancement of the conference theme.

Award Benefits

  • Best Paper Award Certificate
  • Memento / Trophy
  • Special recognition during the Valedictory Session
  • Opportunity for further publication consideration / recommendation in a special issue or associated publication outlet, subject to editorial policies
  • Cash prize, if approved by the Organizing Committee
  • Announcement on the conference website and/or official proceedings communication

Best Oral Presentation Award

The Best Oral Presentation Award recognizes the participant who delivers the most effective, engaging, and academically strong oral presentation during the technical sessions of the conference.

This award emphasizes not only the quality of the research but also the presenter’s ability to communicate the work clearly, professionally, and convincingly to the conference audience.

Eligibility

  • Open to accepted oral presentations presented during the conference.
  • In the case of a multi-author paper, the presenting author will be considered for the award.

Evaluation Criteria

  • clarity, structure, and organization of the presentation;
  • quality and effectiveness of slides / visual aids;
  • communication skills and audience engagement;
  • time management and overall delivery;
  • ability to respond effectively to questions and discussion.

Award Benefits

  • Best Oral Presentation Certificate
  • Memento / Trophy / Medal
  • Recognition during the Valedictory Session
  • Acknowledgement on the conference website and/or official conference media platforms

Best Student Presenter Award

The Best Student Presenter Award is intended to recognize outstanding student participants who demonstrate strong research quality, clarity of thought, and effective presentation skills during the conference.

This award is aimed at encouraging young scholars and emerging researchers to actively engage in research communication and academic excellence.

Eligibility

  • Open to Undergraduate and Postgraduate student participants.
  • The student must be the presenting author of the paper/presentation.
  • Proof of student status may be required at the time of registration or award evaluation.

Evaluation Criteria

  • quality and originality of the research work;
  • clarity of objectives, methodology, and conclusions;
  • confidence, coherence, and effectiveness of presentation;
  • ability to answer questions and participate in discussion.

Award Benefits

  • Best Student Presenter Certificate
  • Memento / Medal / Trophy
  • Recognition during the conference valedictory function
  • Feature in conference highlights / newsletter / website, where applicable
  • Consideration for future student recognition opportunities under conference policy

Award Ceremony

All award recipients will be honored during the Valedictory Session of the International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications”. Awardees may receive a certificate and memento, and any additional benefits will be subject to the approval and policy of the Organizing Committee.

The decision of the Award Evaluation Committee / Organizing Committee regarding nominations, shortlisting, and final selection shall be final.
