Overview

Call for Special Session Proposals

The Organizing Committee of the International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications” warmly invites proposals for Special Sessions that align with the themes, scope, and objectives of the conference. The conference will be held on October 5–6, 2026, and is jointly organized by the International Centre for Basic and Applied Sciences, SIDDHI VINAYAK SHIKSHA SAMITI, India and Siirt University, Turkey.

Special Sessions are intended to provide focused and in-depth discussions on emerging research areas, interdisciplinary themes, and recent advances in mathematics, fractional calculus, computational techniques, and engineering applications. These sessions aim to bring together researchers, academicians, scientists, industry experts, and practitioners working in specialized areas to exchange ideas, present innovative results, and foster meaningful national and international collaborations.

Objectives of Special Sessions

Special Sessions are designed to:

  • highlight cutting-edge research trends and novel developments in mathematics, fractional calculus, and engineering applications;
  • encourage interdisciplinary interaction across mathematics, science, engineering, and technology;
  • promote collaborative research and academic networking among participants from universities, research institutions, and industry;
  • provide a focused platform for specialized discussion beyond the regular conference tracks;
  • stimulate dialogue on emerging mathematical methods, computational models, and real-world engineering problems.

Indicative Topics for Special Sessions

Proposals are invited in areas including, but not limited to, the following themes:

  • Fractional calculus and fractional differential equations
  • Fractional integral operators, transforms, and special functions
  • Mathematical analysis and nonlinear differential equations
  • Numerical and computational methods in fractional systems
  • Dynamical systems, chaos, stability, and bifurcation theory
  • Control theory, optimization, and fractional-order control systems
  • Mathematical modeling in physical sciences and engineering
  • Applications of fractional calculus in fluid mechanics, viscoelasticity, diffusion, and transport phenomena
  • Bio-mathematical, medical, ecological, and epidemiological models
  • Signal processing, image analysis, and intelligent computational methods
  • Data-driven mathematical modeling, artificial intelligence, and machine learning approaches
  • Interdisciplinary applications of mathematics in engineering, technology, and applied sciences
  • Emerging theoretical and applied developments in nonlocal and memory-dependent systems

Guidelines for Special Sessions

  • Each Special Session should normally include a minimum of ten (10) paper presentations.
  • The Special Session Organizer(s) will be responsible for:
    • promoting the session and encouraging high-quality submissions;
    • coordinating the academic review of abstracts/papers in accordance with the conference review standards and timelines;
    • maintaining communication with authors and the conference organizing team regarding session submissions and accepted papers.
  • Session Organizer(s) may serve as Chair / Co-Chair of their Special Session.
  • Subject to conference policy, a registration fee concession / waiver for one paper may be considered for the Session Organizer if the Special Session successfully registers the required number of papers. Final approval of such benefits will rest with the Organizing Committee.

Guidelines for Special Session Proposals

Each Special Session proposal should include the following information:

  1. Title of the Special Session
  2. Brief description of the proposed session, clearly indicating its relevance to the conference theme and objectives
  3. List of specific topics to be covered under the session
  4. Name(s), affiliation(s), and contact details of the Session Organizer(s)
  5. Short bio-note of the Session Organizer(s), highlighting expertise and research contributions in the proposed area

Additional Information

  • Authors intending to submit papers to a Special Session may be asked to send their abstracts/full papers directly to the respective Special Session Organizer(s), with a copy to the official conference email address.
  • After the review process, Session Organizer(s) should forward the final list of accepted papers to the conference organizing team within the deadlines specified by the conference.
  • All papers submitted under Special Sessions will be subject to the same academic and review standards as regular conference submissions.
  • For any queries related to Special Session proposals, prospective organizers may contact the conference secretariat through the official conference email.

The Organizing Committee warmly welcomes proposals for innovative and high-impact Special Sessions that will enrich the scientific program of the conference and contribute to advancing research at the intersection of mathematics, fractional calculus, and engineering applications.
