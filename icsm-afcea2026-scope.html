Call for Papers

Scope of Conference

The International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications” invites original research papers, review articles, and case studies addressing theoretical developments, computational methods, and interdisciplinary applications in mathematics, fractional calculus, and engineering sciences. Prospective authors are encouraged to submit contributions in, but not limited to, the following thematic tracks:

Track 1: Fractional Calculus and Fractional Differential Equations
  • Theory of fractional derivatives and integrals
  • Fractional differential and integro-differential equations
  • Initial and boundary value problems in fractional systems
  • Caputo, Riemann–Liouville, Hadamard, Atangana–Baleanu and related operators
  • Existence, uniqueness, stability, and controllability of fractional models
  • Fractional delay, impulsive, and stochastic differential equations
Track 2: Special Functions, Integral Transforms, and Operator Theory
  • Classical and generalized special functions
  • Integral transforms and operational calculus
  • Fractional integral operators and transform methods
  • Orthogonal polynomials and approximation techniques
  • Operator theory in mathematical analysis
  • Applications of transforms in engineering and physical sciences
Track 3: Numerical and Computational Methods in Fractional Systems
  • Numerical schemes for fractional differential equations
  • Finite difference, finite element, spectral, and meshless methods
  • Computational algorithms for nonlocal and memory-dependent models
  • Error analysis, convergence, and stability of numerical methods
  • Scientific computing and simulation in applied mathematics
  • High-performance computing for complex mathematical models
Track 4: Dynamical Systems, Control, and Optimization
  • Fractional-order dynamical systems
  • Stability, bifurcation, chaos, and synchronization
  • Fractional control systems and robust control methods
  • Optimal control and optimization in engineering systems
  • Network dynamics, complex systems, and decision models
  • Control applications in automation, robotics, and smart technologies
Track 5: Mathematical Modeling in Physical and Engineering Sciences
  • Mathematical models in fluid mechanics, heat transfer, and wave propagation
  • Fractional models in viscoelasticity, diffusion, and transport phenomena
  • Electromagnetic, mechanical, and structural system modeling
  • Applied modeling in civil, electrical, mechanical, and chemical engineering
  • Modeling of nonlinear and multiscale physical systems
  • Analytical and computational approaches to engineering processes
Track 6: Fractional Calculus in Biological, Medical, and Environmental Systems
  • Population dynamics and epidemiological models
  • Fractional bio-mathematical models and biomedical systems
  • Signal processing and imaging in medical applications
  • Ecological, environmental, and climate-related mathematical models
  • Fractional approaches to physiological and neural systems
  • Modeling uncertainty, memory, and hereditary effects in life sciences
Track 7: Data-Driven Mathematics, AI, and Emerging Interdisciplinary Applications
  • Mathematical foundations for data science and intelligent systems
  • Fractional and nonlinear methods in machine learning models
  • Optimization and computational intelligence techniques
  • Mathematical methods in image processing, pattern recognition, and forecasting
  • Applications in finance, economics, logistics, and industrial systems
  • Emerging interdisciplinary applications connecting mathematics, computation, and engineering
Track 8: Pure and Applied Mathematical Analysis
  • Real and complex analysis
  • Functional analysis and fixed point theory
  • Differential equations and boundary value problems
  • Algebra, geometry, topology, and number theory
  • Probability, statistics, and stochastic processes
  • Mathematical methods for applied and interdisciplinary research
Track 9: Recent Advances in Engineering Applications of Mathematics
  • Mathematical tools for smart manufacturing and Industry 4.0
  • Engineering design optimization and reliability analysis
  • Applications in communication systems, sensors, and control devices
  • Mathematical modeling in renewable energy and sustainable technologies
  • Simulation and analysis of engineering networks and infrastructures
  • Case studies on industrial applications of advanced mathematical techniques
Track 10: Interdisciplinary Frontiers in Fractional Calculus
  • Fractional calculus in economics, social systems, and management science
  • Nonlocal models in complex adaptive systems
  • Hybrid mathematical models combining classical and fractional approaches
  • Cross-disciplinary frameworks linking mathematics, computation, and engineering
  • Open problems, emerging theories, and future directions in fractional research

Note: The above tracks are indicative and not exhaustive. Papers addressing other relevant topics aligned with the theme of the conference are also welcome.
