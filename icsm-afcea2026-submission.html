Abstract & Paper Submission

The Organizing Committee of the International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications” invites researchers, academicians, scientists, industry professionals, and research scholars to submit original contributions for presentation at the conference. Submissions are welcome in all areas aligned with the conference themes, including mathematics, fractional calculus, nonlinear systems, computational methods, and engineering applications.

All submissions must be prepared in English and should present original, unpublished work that is not under consideration elsewhere. Contributions may be submitted either for presentation only or for presentation and consideration for publication, subject to the conference review process and publication policies.

Abstract Submission

Participants wishing to present their work at the conference are requested to submit a concise abstract of not more than 300 words. The abstract should clearly state the title of the paper, objective of the study, methodology or theoretical framework, major findings or expected outcomes, and significance of the work.

All abstracts will be evaluated by the scientific committee / reviewers for relevance, originality, and alignment with the scope of the conference. Once an abstract is accepted, authors who wish to publish their work in the conference proceedings or associated indexed publications will be invited to submit the full-length manuscript in accordance with the conference template and submission guidelines. Final communication regarding acceptance will be issued based on the recommendations of the reviewers.

Abstract Submission Guidelines

  • The abstract must be written in English.
  • The abstract should not exceed 300 words.
  • The abstract should include:
    • Title of the paper
    • Author name(s) and affiliation(s)
    • Email address of the corresponding author
    • Keywords
    • A concise summary of the work including purpose, methodology, and key results/contributions

Full-Length Paper Submission

Authors whose abstracts are accepted will be invited to submit a full-length manuscript if they wish their work to be considered for publication in the conference proceedings and/or associated publication outlets, subject to peer review and editorial policies.

The full paper should present original research, theoretical developments, computational studies, methodological innovations, or application-oriented contributions relevant to the conference theme.

Manuscript Preparation Guidelines

Authors are requested to prepare their manuscripts according to the following instructions:

  • Manuscripts must be submitted in MS Word format.
  • Use Times New Roman, 12-point font, 1.5 line spacing, and a single-column layout.
  • The manuscript should preferably contain the following sections:
    1. Title of the Paper
    2. Author(s) Name(s)
    3. Affiliation(s) and Address(es)
    4. Abstract (maximum 300 words)
    5. Keywords
    6. Introduction
    7. Problem Formulation / Mathematical Preliminaries
    8. Methodology / Theoretical Analysis / Experimental Framework
    9. Results and Discussion
    10. Conclusions
    11. Acknowledgements (if applicable)
    12. References

Citation and Reference Style

  • Citations in the text should follow the author–number format [xx] or the citation style prescribed in the conference template.
  • References should be listed in a standard scholarly format. For example:
    A.B. Author, C.D. Author, and E.F. Author, “Title of the Article,” Journal Name, vol. xx, no. xx, pp. xx–xx, year.

Figures and Tables

  • All tables, graphs, figures, and illustrations must be embedded within the manuscript at appropriate positions.
  • Authors should ensure that all figures are clear, properly labeled, and of publication quality.

Important Note for Full Paper Submission

Authors of accepted abstracts are invited to submit their full papers in accordance with the conference template and submission guidelines. All submissions must be original and unpublished and must not be simultaneously submitted to another conference or journal.

All full-length papers will undergo a rigorous peer-review process. Based on reviewer recommendations and editorial decisions, selected papers will be accepted for conference presentation and may be considered for publication in the conference proceedings and/or associated special issues, subject to quality, scope, and publication approval.

At least one author of each accepted paper must register and present the paper at the conference for the work to be included in the official conference program and any eligible publication process.

Certificate Policy

Please note that in the case of a paper with multiple authors, a certificate will be issued only to those authors who have individually registered and attended/presented at the conference in accordance with conference policy. If co-authors do not register or attend, the certificate may be issued only to the presenting / registered author.

Publication and Review Note

Submission of an abstract or full paper implies that the authors agree to the conference review and publication process. Authors are expected to ensure the academic integrity, originality, and proper citation of all sources used in their work. The conference reserves the right to reject submissions that do not comply with the scope, formatting guidelines, ethical standards, or review requirements.
